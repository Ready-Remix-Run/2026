The possible pattern values are:

  • a digit 0-8 meaning the specific value 0-8
  • N means any nonzero digit
  • n means any digit (i.e., this cell has been explored and it is empty)
  • - means blank (unexplored)
  • ? matches any character - including "out of bounds"
  • m means "MARKED"

At least one of the cells in the pattern should be blank (unexplored), so that an action may be performed on it.

An action contains "?" everywhere except that in a place corresponding to a blank cell in the pattern, it contains either a "P" indicating that the cell may safely be probed, or a "M" meaning the cell must be marked.

Welcome to Truffle Hunt

You are searching for truffles – highly prized subterranean mushrooms that are buried in the field above, and must be harvested carefully. You are accompanied by a truffle-sniffing boar, which is remarkable at locating truffles, but will eat them if it actually gets close enough to them.

The field is known to contain 10 truffles. You find truffles by allowing your boar to probe an unmarked spot in the field (by clicking that spot). If the spot contains a truffle, the boar will eat it. You don't want that to happen! If the spot is empty, the boar's sense of smell is keen enough to tell you how many truffles are nearby, i.e., in one of its immediate neighbors. By probing carefully, you should be able to deduce which spots are safe to probe, and which must contain truffles. The game ends when you either isolate all 10 truffles (you win), or accidentally probe a truffle (you lose, because your boar eats the truffle). You may have failed, but at least your boar will love you.

Begin by clicking a cell, which instructs your boar to probe it. The cell will immediately display either an integer showing the number of neighboring cells that contain a truffle, or a red "X", designating that the boar actually found a truffle and ate it. When you find a cell you feel certain contains a truffle, you can right-click on it (or ctrl-click it), and it will be marked in red.

Happy hunting!
